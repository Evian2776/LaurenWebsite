Poetry



Beaten barren fields

Beaten barren fields

Meadows a white goods wasteland

Crows stand sentinel atop telephone wires

Fly cawing over the dumping ground

Fields of cars by the fishing lakes

Litter trails down the garden path

Fields torn up to build houses with nice views

All huddled together as if afraid of where they stand

Bare golf courses where weeds are weeds

Sheep grazing a ragged field

All is green

All will be lost


Lifelike

What makes a tooth into a fang?

Life is in the eye of the beholder.

When do hands become claws?

What breaks a face?

Put pennies on my eyes. Give me to the fire.

What makes a fang into a claw?

Bones sticking out like an exoskeleton.

How long does it take for a face to turn?

When does softness expire?

Put me in a box. Give me to the ground.

What makes a claw into a face?

A soul is reflected in an eye.

Why should I want to be thinner?

What is a soul?

Vultures are circling. Give me to the birds.

What makes a face into a soul?

Bones sticking out like a bird’s wing.

Why don’t I see what I think?

What breaks a life?

Light is fading. Give me to the dark.
